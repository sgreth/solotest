North Village

Belk Hall

Belk Hall, which opened in 2013, was designed to primarily house upper-class students. Belk Hall offers a blend of apartments and suite units. The common areas feature study rooms, casual lounges and community kitchens throughout the building. Its open design encourages interaction while creating a community atmosphere.

Lynch Hall

Lynch Hall, which opened in 2004, houses approximately 500 students and is reserved for new students, many of whom will be enrolled in Learning Communities. Lynch offers suite-style living with 4 students sharing a bathroom and living room. Lynch features lounges/study rooms and laundry facilities available on each floor. The first floor features a classroom, large community room, game room, vending area and kitchen.

Miltimore Hall

Miltimore Hall, which opened in 2011, is located in North Village. Miltimore offers both suite and apartment style units. Community lounges and kitchens, central laundry facilities are located on each floor. Miltimore is LEED certified meaning that the design, construction and use of the building have met independently established criteria for eco-friendliness. As such, this building represents UNC Charlotteâ€™s commitment to be greener.

Wallis Hall

Wallis Hall, which opened in 2000, is located in North Village. Wallis offers both suite and apartment style living options. Community kitchens and laundry facilities are available on the second through fourth floors. Wallis also has study lounges and classrooms on each floor, not to mention a great courtyard! Apartments in Wallis have four private bedrooms,one-and-a-half bathrooms, a living room, a kitchen and a storage closet. Suites in Wallis Hall have either two private bedrooms or four private bedrooms, a full bathroom and a living area.

Witherspoon Hall

Witherspoon Hall, which opened in 1990, offers unique opportunities for cultural and academic engagement. With the International House on the second floor, it's one of the most diverse residential communities. It is also home to the Transfer Student Learning Community. Witherspoon offers suites and apartment style living options. It features a community kitchen on the second floor, and laundry and vending facilities available on the first floor.

South Village

Holshouser Hall

Holshouser Hall was rennovated and reconfigured completely in 2015. Holshouser Hall offers traditional-style doubles featuring two private bathrooms for every three rooms, and suites with two double-occupancy bedrooms, a living room and bathroom. Each floor features multiple lounge and study spaces. Holshouser also offers male and female rooms on all floors. It is the only tower on campus with single user bathrooms which allows for additional flexibility with room assignments.

Hunt Hall

Hunt Hall, which opened in 2013, is a suite-style housing option in South Village. Suites in Hunt have either two or four private bedrooms, a bathroom and a living room. Hunt also features conveniently placed study rooms, game room, community lounges, an atrium with a piano, a welcoming courtyard and outdoor patio area.

Laurel Hall

Laurel Hall, which opened in 2015, is a suite-style housing option in South Village for freshmen. Laurel offers a variety of suite layouts that gives residents the opportunity to share living and bathroom space. Laurel features a classroom, gaming area, community lounges, community kitchens and central laundry facilities on each floor.

Levine Hall

Levine Hall, named in honor of Sandra and Leon Levine, opened in 2016. In addition to more than 400 students, Levine Hall houses students of the Levine Scholars Program and the Honors College, as well as administrative offices for the two programs. Student work-study areas, faculty work stations, private one-on-one spaces and adjacent seminar spaces that can be combined for large functions will be some of the many features of this building. Levine also provides a variety of housing options, including suite and apartment style living.

Sanford Hall

Sanford Hall, which opened in 1969, is located in South Village right by SoVi Dining Hall. Sanford offers traditional style rooms and floor community bathrooms that are shared by no more than twelve residents. Laundry facilities and a community kitchen are available in the basement, and vending is available on the first floor.

Scott Hall

Scott Hall, which was renovated in 2018, is a freshmen-only tower building in South Village that fosters a close-knit community of students who reside in two-person rooms. Each floor features multiple lounge and study spaces, as well as, spa-style community bathrooms and a single user bathroom for increased privacy. The first floor boasts a large kitchen, group and private study rooms, a social lounge that includes game tables and televisions. Scott Hall is also the first hall to feature a movie room with stadium reclining seats and an 80-inch television. Laundry facilities and vending are located in the basement.

East Village

Elm Hall

Elm Hall, which was renovated in 2017, offers a community of apartments for residents who are ready for a more independent lifestyle. Apartments in Elm have four private bedrooms, a bathroom, a living room, a kitchen and a private washer and dryer. Depending on the unit, residents will enjoy a balcony/terrace or additional living room space. In addition to completely renovated apartments, residents also have access to a renovated community lounge, study space and gaming area in Maple Hall.

Greek Village

Each house has a large living/chapter room, dining room, office and full kitchen for common use and sleeps either 28 or 14 residents. Common outdoor spaces serve as a centerpiece for the Greek Community and include a sand volleyball court and picnic pavillion, creating a park-like setting for activities and events. In the houses occupied by Greek lettered organizations, residence is reserved for members of the organization and live-in requirements vary per chapter.

Hawthorn Hall

Hawthorn Hall is home to the Engineering Learning Community. Residents of Hawthorn enjoy being a part of a close-knit community focused on academic success in engineering and related math and science fields. Suites in Hawthorn Hall have two double bedrooms, a bathroom and a living room for four residents. Laundry, vending and kitchen facilities are available in the basement.

Residents of Hawthorn enjoy being a part of a close-knit community focused on academic success in engineering and related math and science fields. Suites in Hawthorn Hall have two double bedrooms, a bathroom and a living room for four residents. Laundry, vending and kitchen facilities are available in the basement.

Oak Hall

Oak Hall, which was renovated in 2015, is located in East Village close to the academic center of campus and gives residents quick access to classes, the Student Union and the Student Activity Center. Oak Hall offers suite style living options with two bedrooms, a bathroom and a living room. Laundry, vending and kitchen facilities are located on the first floor. In addition, residents also have access to community lounges, study spaces and gaming areas throughout the building.

Pine Hall

Pine Hall, which was renovated in 2017, offers a community of apartments for residents who are ready for a more independent lifestyle. Apartments in Pine have four private bedrooms, a bathroom, a living room, a kitchen and a private washer and dryer. Depending on the unit, residents will enjoy a balcony/terrace or additional living room space. In addition to completely renovated apartments, residents also have access to a renovated community lounge, study space and gaming area in Maple Hall.
